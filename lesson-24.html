Part 2: Writing About History

Your Writing Guides

When writing about historical events, your writing should be:

  1. Informative: Provide accurate and relevant historical details.
  2. Analytical: Explain the significance and impact of the event.
  3. Concise: Summarize complex information effectively.
  4. Structured: Present information in a clear, chronological, or thematic order.

Writing Structure: The Research Summary of a Historical Event

This structure will help you write a clear and informative summary:

  • Title: Clear and descriptive (e.g., "The Fall of the Berlin Wall: A Symbol of Change").
  • Introduction (1 paragraph):
  • Hook: Start with a general statement about the importance of historical moments.
  • Background: Introduce the historical event you will summarize (name, date, location).
  • Thesis Statement (or controlling idea): Briefly state the event's main significance or its primary impact.
  • Example Thesis: "The fall of the Berlin Wall in 1989 marked a pivotal turning point in the Cold War, symbolizing the collapse of communist regimes and paving the way for German reunification, leaving a profound legacy of freedom and unity."

  • Body Paragraph 1: Causes and Key Events (1 paragraph):
  • Topic Sentence: Introduce the factors that led to the event and its key occurrences.
  • Elaboration: Briefly describe the historical context, the catalyst for the event, and the main actions that took place.
  • Example: "Decades of political tension and economic disparity contributed to growing unrest in East Germany. The immediate catalyst for the Wall's collapse was a miscommunication during a press conference, which triggered a spontaneous gathering of citizens at the border crossings."
  • Body Paragraph 2: Immediate and Lasting Impact (1 paragraph):
  • Topic Sentence: Discuss the immediate consequences and the lasting effect or legacy of the event.
  • Elaboration: Explain how the event shaped subsequent developments, both locally and globally.
  • Example: "The immediate consequence was the joyous dismantling of the Wall, followed rapidly by the reunification of Germany. This event set a precedent for other nations seeking liberation, and its legacy continues to influence geopolitical relations today."
  • Conclusion (1 paragraph):
  • Summarize: Briefly restate the event's significance and its primary impacts.
  • Reiterate Importance: Emphasize why this event remains important to study.
  • Final Thought: Offer a reflective statement about the lessons learned from history.

Writing Practice: Your Historical Summary

  1. Outline Your Summary: Choose a specific historical event that you can research and summarize its causes and impact (e.g., the invention of the printing press, the Industrial Revolution, a major civil rights movement, the first moon landing). Create a quick outline for a short summary (2-3 paragraphs) following the structure above.
  2. Draft Your Summary: Start writing your historical summary. Focus on:
  • Providing accurate and concise information.
  • Clearly explaining cause and effect.
  • Using formal historical vocabulary.
  • Highlighting the event's significance.
