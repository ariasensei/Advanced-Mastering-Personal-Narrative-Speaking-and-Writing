Part 1: Speaking About Problems and Solutions

Your Speaking Guides

When discussing problems and decisions, these guides will help you:

  1. Define the Problem: Clearly state what the issue is.
  2. Brainstorm Solutions: Think of multiple ways to address the problem.
  3. Evaluate Pros and Cons: Consider the advantages and disadvantages of each solution.
  4. Justify Your Choice: Explain why you believe a particular solution is best.
  5. Use Logical Connectors: Link your ideas in a clear, cause-and-effect manner.

Vocabulary Builder: Words for Problem-Solving and Decision-Making

Let's build your word bank! Remember to write these down in your notebook, and add any new ones you learn!

Problem Identification & Analysis:

  • Challenge: A difficult task or problem.
  • Dilemma: A situation in which a difficult choice has to be made between two or more alternatives.
  • Obstacle: A thing that blocks one's way or prevents progress.
  • Issue: An important topic or problem for debate or discussion.
  • Root cause: The fundamental reason for the occurrence of a problem.
  • Consequence: A result or effect of an action or condition.
  • Implication: The conclusion that can be drawn from something although it is not explicitly stated.
  • Analyze: To examine in detail the structure of something.
  • Assess: To evaluate or estimate the nature, ability, or quality of.
  • Identify: To establish or indicate who or what someone or something is.

Solution Generation & Evaluation:

  • Solution: A means of solving a problem or dealing with a difficult situation.
  • Alternative: One of two or more available possibilities.
  • Strategy: A plan of action designed to achieve a long-term or overall aim.
  • Approach: A way of dealing with something.
  • Feasible: Possible to do easily or conveniently.
  • Viable: Capable of working successfully; feasible.
  • Effective: Successful in producing a desired or intended result.
  • Pros and cons: The arguments for and against something.
  • Trade-off: A balance achieved between two desirable but incompatible features; a compromise.
  • Mitigate: To make less severe, serious, or painful.

Decision-Making & Justification:

  • Decide: To come to a resolution in the mind as a result of consideration.
  • Choose: To select freely after consideration.
  • Prioritize: To determine the order for dealing with (items or tasks) according to their relative importance.
  • Justify: To show or prove to be right or reasonable.
  • Rationale: The underlying reason.
  • Outcome: The way a thing turns out; a consequence.

Grammar Focus: Expressing Conditions and Justifications

These structures will help you discuss problems and decisions logically:

Using Conditional Sentences (Type 1, 2, and 3):

  • Type 1 (Real/Possible): If [present simple], [will + base verb]. (e.g., "If we implement this strategy, we will see positive outcomes.")
  • Type 2 (Unreal/Hypothetical): If [past simple], [would + base verb]. (e.g., "If I were facing this dilemma, I would prioritize the long-term benefits.")
  • Type 3 (Past Unreal): If [past perfect], [would have + past participle]. (e.g., "If they had identified the root cause earlier, they could have mitigated the consequences.")

Expressing Cause and Effect (Review & Expansion):

  • [Cause] leads to [effect].
  • [Effect] is a consequence of [cause].
  • Due to [reason], [result].
  • As a result, ...
  • Therefore, ...

Justifying Choices:

  • I believe this is the best solution because...
  • The rationale behind this decision is that...
  • This approach is justified by...
  • The primary reason for choosing this is...

Speaking Practice: Solving a Hypothetical Problem

  1. Choose a Hypothetical Problem: Imagine a common problem faced by students or in your community (e.g., too much screen time, lack of school resources, community waste problem).
  2. Define the Problem: Clearly state the problem and its potential consequences.
  3. Brainstorm Solutions: Suggest 2-3 alternatives or strategies.
  4. Evaluate: Discuss the pros and cons of each solution. Consider which is most feasible or effective.
  5. Make a Decision & Justify: Choose the best solution and explain your rationale.
  6. Discuss: Practice discussing this problem and your proposed solution. Set a timer for 2-3 minutes for this practice. Try to use:
  • At least 3-4 new vocabulary words from the list.
  • At least 1-2 conditional sentences.
  • Phrases to justify your choice.
