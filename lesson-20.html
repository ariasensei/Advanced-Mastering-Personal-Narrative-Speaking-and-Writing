Part 1: Speaking About Technology

Your Speaking Guides

When discussing technology and society, these guides will help you:

  1. Present Both Sides: Be able to discuss both the advantages and disadvantages of a technology.
  2. Use Specific Examples: Refer to particular technologies or applications to support your points.
  3. Consider Future Implications: Think about what a technology might lead to in the long term.
  4. Use Argumentative Language: Employ phrases for expressing agreement, disagreement, and concession.
  5. Speak with Precision: Use accurate terms when describing technological concepts.

Vocabulary Builder: Words for Technology and Society

Let's build your word bank! Remember to write these down in your notebook, and add any new ones you learn!

Technological Concepts:

  • Innovation: A new method, idea, product, etc.
  • Automation: The use of largely automatic equipment in a system of manufacturing or other production process.
  • Artificial Intelligence (AI): The theory and development of computer systems able to perform tasks that normally require human intelligence.
  • Digital literacy: The ability to find, evaluate, create, and communicate information using digital technologies.
  • Connectivity: The state of being connected or interconnected.
  • Virtual reality (VR): The computer-generated simulation of a three-dimensional image or environment that can be interacted with.
  • Augmented reality (AR): A technology that superimposes a computer-generated image on a user's view of the real world.
  • Cybersecurity: The state of being protected against the criminal or unauthorized use of electronic data.
  • Algorithm: A process or set of rules to be followed in calculations or other problem-solving operations.
  • Data privacy: The right of an individual to have control over their personal data.

Societal Impact & Evaluation:

  • Impact: The effect or influence of one person, thing, or action, on another.
  • Consequence: A result or effect of an action or condition.
  • Benefit: An advantage or profit gained from something.
  • Drawback: A disadvantage or problem.
  • Ethical implications: Moral considerations related to a technology's use.
  • Efficiency: The state or quality of being efficient.
  • Accessibility: The quality of being able to be reached or entered.
  • Dependence: The state of relying on or being controlled by someone or something else.
  • Disruption: Disturbance or interruption of an event, process, or activity.
  • Transformation: A dramatic change in form or appearance.

Grammar Focus: Debating and Explaining Impact

These structures will help you effectively debate and explain the impact of technology:

Expressing Advantages and Disadvantages:

  • One significant benefit of [technology] is that...
  • However, a major drawback is...
  • On the one hand, [advantage]. On the other hand, [disadvantage].
  • While [advantage], it also presents [disadvantage].

Discussing Cause and Effect (Review & Expansion):

  • [Technology] has led to [impact].
  • The rise of [technology] has resulted in [consequence].
  • As a consequence of [technology], we observe [effect].
  • This innovation facilitates [positive outcome].
  • It can hinder [negative outcome].

Expressing Agreement/Disagreement (Politely):

  • I agree with the point that...
  • I see your point, but I also believe that...
  • While that may be true, it's important to consider...
  • I respectfully disagree with the idea that...

Speaking Practice: Debating Technology's Impact

  1. Choose a Technology: Select a specific technology that has a significant impact on society (e.g., smartphones, social media, AI, self-driving cars).
  2. Identify Pros and Cons: Brainstorm at least two benefits and two drawbacks of this technology.
  3. Discuss its Impact:
  • How has it transformed daily life?
  • What are its ethical implications?
  • How has it affected connectivity or digital literacy?
  1. Debate: Practice discussing the chosen technology, presenting both its advantages and disadvantages. Set a timer for 2-3 minutes for this practice. Try to use:
  • At least 3-4 new vocabulary words from the list.
  • Phrases for expressing pros and cons.
  • Sentences that show cause and effect.
