Homework Assignments

To help you master discussing and writing about global issues:

  1. Speaking Practice:
  • Discuss an environmental challenge and its solution with a family member or friend.
  • Try to use at least 5 new words or phrases related to global issues.
  1. Vocabulary Journal:
  • Review your notebook. Choose your top 5 favorite new words from today and write one original sentence for each, related to environmental issues.
  • Try to make at least two of these sentences complex (using "although," "because," "in order to," etc.).
  1. Complete the Essay:
  • Finish writing your short essay on an environmental challenge and its solution (aim for about 250-350 words).
  • Focus on clear arguments, good structure, formal language, and include at least 10-12 of the new environmental terms and complex sentences.
  1. Proofread & Edit:
  • Carefully proofread your essay for grammar, spelling, punctuation, and clarity.
  • Read it aloud to catch awkward phrasing or errors.
  1. Vocabulary Integration:
  • Review all the new words and phrases in your notebook from both Lesson 1 and Lesson 2.
  • Create 3 new sentences that combine vocabulary from both 'Personal Narratives' and 'Environmental Issues' topics. (e.g., "My aspiration to address climate change stems from a memorable personal experience I had last summer.")

Remember your notebook! Continue to use it actively. Write down new words, good sample sentences, and any tips that help you improve your speaking and writing. This will be a valuable resource for your test!

Lesson 3: Analyzing Media

Hello students! In this lesson, we're going to dive into the world of media analysis. This means learning how to understand and discuss different types of media, like news, social media, and advertisements, and how they influence us. By the end of this lesson, you'll be able to critically evaluate media messages and express your opinions clearly.
