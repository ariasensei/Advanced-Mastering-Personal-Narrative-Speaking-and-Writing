Homework Assignments

To help you practice and remember what you've learned:

  1. Speaking Practice:
  • Retell your personal experience and your aspirations to a family member or friend.
  • Try to use at least 5 new words or phrases we discussed today.
  1. Vocabulary Journal:
  • Review all the words and phrases in your notebook.
  • Choose your top 5 favorite new words and write one original sentence for each, related to personal experiences or future goals.
  1. Complete the Narrative:
  • Finish writing your personal narrative (aim for about 200-300 words).
  • Make sure to check for clear sentence structure, correct grammar, and try to include at least 7-10 of the new words and phrases from our list.
  1. Future Aspirations Paragraph:
  • Write a separate paragraph (5-7 sentences) describing your top 2-3 future aspirations, explaining why they are important to you.
  • Use at least 3 new aspiration-related words.
  1. Proofread: Read your narrative and aspirations paragraph aloud to yourself. This helps you catch awkward phrasing or errors.

Remember your notebook! This is your personal dictionary and idea bank. Make sure to write down all new words, good sample sentences, and any writing tips you find helpful. The more you use it, the more you'll remember!

Lesson 2: Exploring Global Issues

Hello students! In this lesson, we're going to expand our English skills by discussing important global issues, specifically focusing on environmental challenges and their solutions. This will help you learn how to talk and write about complex topics using more formal and academic language. By the end, you'll be able to identify key environmental problems and propose ways to solve them!
