Homework Assignments

To help you master discussing and writing about problem-solving:

  1. Speaking Practice:
  • Identify a small problem you face in your daily life (e.g., managing homework, balancing activities).
  • Discuss this problem with a family member or friend, propose 2-3 alternatives, and explain your chosen strategy, using at least 5 new words or phrases from this lesson.
  1. Vocabulary Journal:
  • Review your notebook. Choose your top 5 favorite new words from today and write one original sentence for each, related to problem-solving.
  • Try to make at least two of these sentences use conditional structures or justification phrases.
  1. Complete the Argumentative Essay:
  • Finish writing your short argumentative essay on a community issue (aim for about 250-350 words).
  • Focus on clear problem definition, a well-supported solution, and include at least 10-12 of the new problem-solving terms and logical connectors.
  1. Proofread & Edit:
  • Carefully proofread your essay for grammar, spelling, punctuation, and clarity.
  • Read it aloud to catch awkward phrasing or errors.
  1. Decision Analysis:
  • Think about a recent important decision you had to make (e.g., choosing a subject, joining a club).
  • In your notebook, briefly list the pros and cons you considered, and explain your rationale for the final outcome.

Remember your notebook! Continue to use it actively. Write down new words, good sample sentences, and any tips that help you improve your speaking and writing. This will be a valuable resource for your test!

Lesson 6: Health and Well-being

Hello students! In this lesson, we're going to explore the important topic of health and well-being. This includes discussing physical, mental, and emotional health, and understanding practices that contribute to a balanced life. By the end of this lesson, you'll be able to talk about healthy habits and write informative pieces on health-related topics.
