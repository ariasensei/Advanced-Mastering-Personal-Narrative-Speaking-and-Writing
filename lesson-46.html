Homework Assignments

To help you master discussing and writing about academic skills:

  1. Speaking Practice:
  • Find a short news article about a new scientific discovery or a social study.
  • Summarize its main findings for a family member or friend, and then offer a brief critique of its evidence or implications, using at least 5 new words or phrases from this lesson.
  1. Vocabulary Journal:
  • Review your notebook. Choose your top 5 favorite new words from today and write one original sentence for each, related to academic work.
  • Try to make at least two of these sentences use structures for presenting findings or critiquing arguments.
  1. Complete the Summary and Critique:
  • Finish writing your short summary and critique of an academic article (aim for about 250-350 words).
  • Focus on accurate summarization, insightful critique, and include at least 10-12 of the new academic terms and analytical phrases.
  1. Proofread & Edit:
  • Carefully proofread your essay for grammar, spelling, punctuation, and clarity.
  • Read it aloud to catch awkward phrasing or errors.
  1. Critical Reading Practice:
  • Choose a short informative text (e.g., a Wikipedia entry on a complex topic, a short essay).
  • In your notebook, identify the author's main claim or conclusion. Then, list 2 strengths and 1 weakness of their argument or evidence.

Remember your notebook! Continue to use it actively. Write down new words, good sample sentences, and any tips that help you improve your speaking and writing. This will be a valuable resource for your test!
