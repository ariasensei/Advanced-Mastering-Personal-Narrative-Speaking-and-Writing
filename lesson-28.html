Homework Assignments

To help you master discussing and writing about arts and literature:

  1. Speaking Practice:
  • Choose a favorite song or a short film clip. Discuss it with a family member or friend.
  • Explain what mood or message it conveys, and what specific elements (lyrics, visuals, music) contribute to its impact, using at least 5 new words or phrases from this lesson.
  1. Vocabulary Journal:
  • Review your notebook. Choose your top 5 favorite new words from today and write one original sentence for each, related to arts or literature.
  • Try to make at least two of these sentences use structures for attributing meaning/effect or supporting interpretations.
  1. Complete the Literary Analysis:
  • Finish writing your short literary analysis (aim for about 250-350 words).
  • Focus on clear interpretation, strong textual evidence, and include at least 10-12 of the new arts/literature terms and analytical phrases.
  1. Proofread & Edit:
  • Carefully proofread your analysis for grammar, spelling, punctuation, and clarity.
  • Read it aloud to catch awkward phrasing or errors.
  1. Art/Literary Exploration:
  • Find a new piece of art (painting, sculpture, song) or a short poem/story you haven't encountered before.
  • In your notebook, briefly describe it and jot down your initial interpretation of its theme or mood.

Remember your notebook! Continue to use it actively. Write down new words, good sample sentences, and any tips that help you improve your speaking and writing. This will be a valuable resource for your test!

Lesson 10: Career Exploration

Hello students! In this lesson, we're going to delve into career exploration. This means thinking about future professions, understanding different industries, and identifying your own skills and interests to guide your career path. By the end of this lesson, you'll be able to discuss various career options and write reflective essays on your future professional interests.
