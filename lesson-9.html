Part 2: Writing About Media

Your Writing Guides

When writing about media analysis, your writing should be:

  1. Analytical: Break down the topic into parts and examine them.
  2. Argumentative: Present a clear opinion or thesis and support it with evidence.
  3. Structured: Follow an essay format with clear paragraphs.
  4. Objective (where appropriate): Present facts and analysis fairly, even when expressing an opinion.

Writing Structure: The Opinion Piece on Media Influence

This structure will help you write a clear and persuasive opinion piece:

  • Introduction (1 paragraph):
  • Hook: Start with a general statement about media's role in society.
  • Background: Briefly introduce the specific aspect of media you'll discuss (e.g., social media's impact, news bias).
  • Thesis Statement: State your main argument or opinion about the influence of this media.
  • Example Thesis: "While social media offers unprecedented connectivity, its pervasive nature often manipulates public perception through the spread of disinformation, necessitating critical evaluation from users."
  • Body Paragraph 1: Problem/Observation (1 paragraph):
  • Topic Sentence: Introduce a specific problem or observation related to your thesis (e.g., how social media spreads disinformation).
  • Elaboration: Provide examples and explain how this problem occurs, using relevant vocabulary. Discuss its effects.
  • Example: "One significant concern is the credibility of information on online platforms. Users often encounter content that lacks verifiable sources, which can influence their understanding of complex issues."
  • Body Paragraph 2: Impact/Consequence (1 paragraph):
  • Topic Sentence: Discuss the impact or consequences of the problem identified in the previous paragraph.
  • Elaboration: Explain why this impact is significant. You can offer solutions or suggest ways to mitigate the negative effects.
  • Example: "This unchecked flow of information leads to a society where distinguishing fact from propaganda becomes increasingly challenging. Consequently, individuals may make decisions based on inaccurate data."
  • Conclusion (1 paragraph):
  • Summarize: Briefly restate your main points in different words.
  • Restate Thesis: Rephrase your thesis statement.
  • Final Thought/Call to Action: End with a strong statement about the importance of media literacy or a call for responsible media consumption.

Writing Practice: Your Opinion Piece

  1. Outline Your Essay: Choose a specific aspect of media influence that you feel strongly about (e.g., the impact of news bias, how advertising affects teenagers, the role of social media in shaping opinions). Create a quick outline for a short essay (2-3 paragraphs) following the structure above.
  2. Draft Your Essay: Start writing your opinion piece. Focus on:
  • Having a clear thesis statement.
  • Organizing your ideas into paragraphs.
  • Using formal and analytical vocabulary.
  • Supporting your opinions with reasoning and examples.
