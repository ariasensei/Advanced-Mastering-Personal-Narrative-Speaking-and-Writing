Homework Assignments

To help you master discussing and writing about global citizenship:

  1. Speaking Practice:
  • Discuss with a family member or friend how a local action (e.g., community clean-up, school fundraiser for a cause) can contribute to a broader global goal (e.g., environmental conservation, poverty reduction).
  • Try to use at least 5 new words or phrases related to global citizenship.
  1. Vocabulary Journal:
  • Review your notebook. Choose your top 5 favorite new words from today and write one original sentence for each, related to global issues.
  • Try to make at least two of these sentences use structures for expressing obligation or persuasive connectors.
  1. Complete the Persuasive Essay:
  • Finish writing your short persuasive essay on a global initiative (aim for about 250-350 words).
  • Focus on clear arguments, strong justifications, and include at least 10-12 of the new global citizenship terms and persuasive phrases.
  1. Proofread & Edit:
  • Carefully proofread your essay for grammar, spelling, punctuation, and clarity.
  • Read it aloud to catch awkward phrasing or errors.
  1. Global News Scan:
  • Read or watch a news report about a current global event (e.g., a humanitarian crisis, an international summit).
  • In your notebook, jot down how this event demonstrates interconnectedness and what responsibilities global citizens might have regarding it.

Remember your notebook! Continue to use it actively. Write down new words, good sample sentences, and any tips that help you improve your speaking and writing. This will be a valuable resource for your test!

Lesson 12: Personal Growth and Resilience

Hello students! In this lesson, we're going to explore personal growth and resilience. This means reflecting on experiences that have helped you grow, understanding how to overcome challenges, and developing mental strength. By the end of this lesson, you'll be able to share stories of personal development and write reflective essays on your journey of growth.
