Homework Assignments

To help you master discussing and writing about media:

  1. Speaking Practice:
  • Discuss a recent news event or a popular social media trend with a family member or friend.
  • Try to analyze its message and potential impact, using at least 5 new words or phrases related to media analysis.
  1. Vocabulary Journal:
  • Review your notebook. Choose your top 5 favorite new words from today and write one original sentence for each, related to media.
  • Try to make at least two of these sentences complex or use a conditional structure.
  1. Complete the Opinion Piece:
  • Finish writing your short opinion piece on media influence (aim for about 250-350 words).
  • Focus on clear arguments, good structure, formal language, and include at least 10-12 of the new media analysis terms and complex sentences.
  1. Proofread & Edit:
  • Carefully proofread your essay for grammar, spelling, punctuation, and clarity.
  • Read it aloud to catch awkward phrasing or errors.
  1. Media Observation:
  • For the next few days, pay close attention to one type of media (e.g., TV commercials, online news headlines, a specific Instagram account).
  • In your notebook, jot down 3-5 observations about how it tries to influence its target audience or if you notice any bias.

Remember your notebook! Continue to use it actively. Write down new words, good sample sentences, and any tips that help you improve your speaking and writing. This will be a valuable resource for your test!

Lesson 4: Cultural Diversity

Hello students! In this lesson, we're going to explore the rich topic of cultural diversity. This means learning how to discuss different cultures, traditions, and perspectives with respect and understanding. By the end of this lesson, you'll be able to share insights about your own culture and appreciate the nuances of others.
