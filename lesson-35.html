Part 1: Speaking About Personal Growth

Your Speaking Guides

When discussing personal growth and resilience, these guides will help you:

  1. Be Reflective: Think deeply about your experiences and what you learned.
  2. Be Honest: Share genuine feelings and insights.
  3. Focus on Lessons Learned: Emphasize the positive takeaways from challenges.
  4. Use Descriptive Language: Paint a picture of your emotional and mental journey.
  5. Speak with Sincerity and Optimism: Convey your journey of development.

Vocabulary Builder: Words for Personal Growth and Resilience

Let's build your word bank! Remember to write these down in your notebook, and add any new ones you learn!

Growth & Development:

  • Personal growth: The process of improving oneself through new experiences and learning.
  • Self-awareness: Conscious knowledge of one's own character, feelings, motives, and desires.
  • Maturity: The state, fact, or period of being mature.
  • Development: The process of developing or being developed.
  • Transformation: A dramatic change in form or appearance.
  • Perspective: A particular attitude toward or way of regarding something; a point of view.
  • Mindset: The established set of attitudes held by someone.
  • Adaptability: The quality of being able to adjust to new conditions.
  • Learning curve: The rate of a person's progress in gaining experience or new skills.
  • Comfort zone: A situation where one feels safe or at ease.

Resilience & Overcoming Challenges:

  • Resilience: The capacity to recover quickly from difficulties; toughness.
  • Adversity: Difficulties; misfortune.
  • Challenge: A call to someone to participate in a competitive situation or fight to prove their ability. (Also, a difficult task or problem).
  • Obstacle: A thing that blocks one's way or prevents or hinders progress.
  • Setback: A reversal or check in progress.
  • Overcome: To succeed in dealing with (a problem or difficulty).
  • Perseverance: Persistence in doing something despite difficulty or delay in achieving success.
  • Grit: Courage and resolve; strength of character.
  • Cope with: To deal effectively with something difficult.
  • Bounce back: To recover quickly from a setback or disappointment.
  • Strengthen: To make or become stronger.

Grammar Focus: Narrating Challenges and Expressing Lessons Learned

These structures will help you effectively narrate personal growth experiences:

Describing Challenges:

  • I faced a significant challenge when...
  • It was an obstacle that required me to...
  • I encountered a setback that taught me...
  • The adversity I experienced involved...

Expressing Lessons Learned:

  • Through this experience, I learned the importance of...
  • It taught me how to [skill/attitude].
  • I realized that [insight].
  • This experience helped me to develop [quality].
  • It strengthened my [aspect].

Using Past Tenses for Narrative:

  • Simple Past: For main events in sequence (e.g., "I encountered a problem. I decided to try a new approach.").
  • Past Continuous: For background actions (e.g., "While I was struggling, I was also seeking advice.").
  • Past Perfect: For actions completed before another past action (e.g., "I had never faced such difficulty before I started this project.").

Speaking Practice: Sharing Experiences of Growth

  1. Choose a Growth Experience: Think about a time when you faced a challenge, adversity, or a setback that ultimately led to personal growth.
  2. Narrate the Experience:
  • What was the challenge?
  • How did you initially cope with it?
  • What actions did you take?
  • What was the outcome?
  1. Reflect on Lessons Learned: What did you learn about yourself? How did you strengthen your resilience or adaptability? How did your perspective or mindset change?
  2. Discuss: Practice sharing your experience of growth. Set a timer for 2-3 minutes for this practice. Try to use:
  • At least 3-4 new vocabulary words from the list.
  • At least 1-2 sentences expressing lessons learned.
  • A combination of past tenses to tell your story.
