Part 2: Writing About Problems and Solutions

Your Writing Guides

When writing about problem-solving, your writing should be:

  1. Analytical: Clearly define the problem and break it down.
  2. Solution-Oriented: Focus on proposing and evaluating solutions.
  3. Logical: Present your arguments and justifications in a clear, step-by-step manner.
  4. Persuasive: Convince the reader that your proposed solution is the most effective.

Writing Structure: The Argumentative Essay on a Community Issue

This structure will help you write a clear and persuasive essay proposing a solution to a community issue:

  • Introduction (1 paragraph):
  • Hook: Start with a general statement about challenges faced by communities.
  • Background: Introduce a specific problem in your community (e.g., littering, lack of public spaces, traffic congestion).
  • Thesis Statement: Clearly state the problem and your main proposed solution.
  • Example Thesis: "The pervasive issue of plastic litter in our local park poses a significant challenge to public health and environmental aesthetics, necessitating the implementation of a comprehensive community recycling program."
  • Body Paragraph 1: Defining the Problem (1 paragraph):
  • Topic Sentence: Elaborate on the chosen community problem.
  • Elaboration: Provide details about the problem, its root causes, and its negative consequences for the community. Use descriptive language.
  • Example: "The accumulation of plastic waste in our park is not merely an aesthetic concern; it leads to environmental degradation and health risks. This issue stems from inadequate waste management and a lack of public awareness."
  • Body Paragraph 2: Proposing and Justifying a Solution (1 paragraph):
  • Topic Sentence: Introduce your proposed solution(s).
  • Elaboration: Explain how your solution would work, why it is feasible and effective, and what positive outcomes it would bring. Use phrases for justification.
  • Example: "One viable solution is to establish a community-led recycling initiative. This approach is justified by its potential to significantly mitigate waste, foster community engagement, and promote sustainable practices."
  • Conclusion (1 paragraph):
  • Summarize: Briefly restate the problem and your proposed solution.
  • Restate Thesis: Rephrase your thesis statement.
  • Final Thought/Call to Action: End with a strong statement about the importance of collective action or the positive future if the solution is adopted.

Writing Practice: Your Community Issue Essay

  1. Outline Your Essay: Choose a specific problem in your school or local community that you believe needs a solution. Create a quick outline for a short essay (2-3 paragraphs) following the structure above.
  2. Draft Your Essay: Start writing your argumentative essay. Focus on:
  • Clearly defining the problem.
  • Proposing a well-reasoned solution.
  • Using logical connectors and justification phrases.
  • Incorporating the new vocabulary.
