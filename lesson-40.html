Homework Assignments

To help you master discussing and writing about ethics and morality:

  1. Speaking Practice:
  • Discuss with a family member or friend a time when you or someone you know had to make a difficult ethical decision.
  • Explain the dilemma and the principles that guided the choice, using at least 5 new words or phrases from this lesson.
  1. Vocabulary Journal:
  • Review your notebook. Choose your top 5 favorite new words from today and write one original sentence for each, related to ethics.
  • Try to make at least two of these sentences use structures for presenting a dilemma or justifying a moral choice.
  1. Complete the Argumentative Essay:
  • Finish writing your short argumentative essay on a moral issue (aim for about 250-350 words).
  • Focus on clear arguments, strong ethical reasoning, and include at least 10-12 of the new ethics/morality terms and relevant phrases.
  1. Proofread & Edit:
  • Carefully proofread your essay for grammar, spelling, punctuation, and clarity.
  • Read it aloud to catch awkward phrasing or errors.
  1. Ethical Reflection:
  • In your notebook, briefly describe a situation (real or hypothetical) where empathy or compassion played a crucial role in an ethical decision. Explain why.

Remember your notebook! Continue to use it actively. Write down new words, good sample sentences, and any tips that help you improve your speaking and writing. This will be a valuable resource for your test!

Lesson 14: Innovation and Future Trends

Hello students! In this lesson, we're going to explore the exciting world of innovation and future trends. This means discussing new ideas, technological advancements, and societal shifts that are shaping our future. By the end of this lesson, you'll be able to speculate about future possibilities and write engaging speculative essays.
