Part 2: Writing About Global Issues

Your Writing Guides

When writing about global issues, your writing should be:

  1. Structured: Follow a clear essay format.
  2. Formal: Use appropriate academic language.
  3. Argumentative: Present a clear problem and propose solutions, supporting your ideas.
  4. Objective: Focus on facts and logical reasoning, rather than just emotions.

Writing Structure: The Argumentative Essay

This structure will help you write a clear and persuasive essay about a global issue:

  • Introduction (1 paragraph):
  • Hook: Start with an interesting fact or general statement about environmental issues.
  • Background: Briefly explain the general problem.
  • Thesis Statement: This is your main argument. It should clearly state the specific environmental challenge you'll discuss and your main proposed solution.
  • Example Thesis: "While plastic pollution poses a significant threat to marine ecosystems, implementing comprehensive waste management and promoting sustainable alternatives are crucial steps towards mitigation."
  • Body Paragraph 1: The Problem (1 paragraph):
  • Topic Sentence: Introduce the specific environmental challenge you chose.
  • Elaboration: Explain the challenge in more detail. Use facts, statistics (if you have them), and discuss its causes and effects.
  • Example: "One of the most pressing environmental challenges today is deforestation, which leads to significant biodiversity loss and contributes to climate change."
  • Body Paragraph 2: The Solution (1 paragraph):
  • Topic Sentence: Introduce your proposed solution(s).
  • Elaboration: Explain how your solution works and why it's effective. Discuss who should be involved (individuals, governments, technology).
  • Example: "Implementing reforestation programs is a vital solution. Furthermore, governments must enact stricter policies to prevent illegal logging."
  • Conclusion (1 paragraph):
  • Summarize: Briefly restate your main points in different words.
  • Restate Thesis: Rephrase the thesis statement.
  • Final Thought/Call to Action: End with a strong statement about the importance of addressing the issue.

Writing Practice: Your Environmental Essay

  1. Outline Your Essay: Choose one specific environmental challenge that interests you. Create a quick outline for a short essay (2-3 paragraphs) following the structure above.
  2. Draft Your Essay: Start writing your essay. Focus on:
  • Having a clear thesis statement.
  • Organizing your ideas into paragraphs.
  • Using the formal vocabulary and complex sentences we discussed.
  • Explaining the problem and proposing solutions.
