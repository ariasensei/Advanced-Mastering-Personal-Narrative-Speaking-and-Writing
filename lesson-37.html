Homework Assignments

To help you master discussing and writing about personal growth and resilience:

  1. Speaking Practice:
  • Discuss with a family member or friend a time when you had to adapt to a new situation (e.g., new school, new hobby).
  • Explain how you coped with it and what you learned about your adaptability or resilience, using at least 5 new words or phrases from this lesson.
  1. Vocabulary Journal:
  • Review your notebook. Choose your top 5 favorite new words from today and write one original sentence for each, related to personal growth.
  • Try to make at least two of these sentences use structures for expressing lessons learned or describing challenges.
  1. Complete the Reflective Essay:
  • Finish writing your short reflective essay on personal growth (aim for about 250-350 words).
  • Focus on genuine reflection, clear narrative, and include at least 10-12 of the new growth and resilience terms and relevant phrases.
  1. Proofread & Edit:
  • Carefully proofread your essay for grammar, spelling, punctuation, and clarity.
  • Read it aloud to catch awkward phrasing or errors.
  1. Growth Reflection:
  • In your notebook, write down 3 qualities you would like to develop in yourself over the next year (e.g., patience, discipline, creativity).
  • Briefly explain why these qualities are important to you and one small step you can take to foster each.

Remember your notebook! Continue to use it actively. Write down new words, good sample sentences, and any tips that help you improve your speaking and writing. This will be a valuable resource for your test!

Lesson 13: Ethics and Morality

Hello students! In this lesson, we're going to delve into the complex topics of ethics and morality. This means learning how to discuss right and wrong, analyze ethical dilemmas, and articulate your own moral reasoning. By the end of this lesson, you'll be able to engage in thoughtful discussions about ethical issues and write argumentative essays on moral questions.
