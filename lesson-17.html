Part 1: Speaking About Health

Your Speaking Guides

When discussing health and well-being, these guides will help you:

  1. Be Informative: Share accurate and helpful information.
  2. Use Specific Examples: Illustrate your points with concrete healthy practices.
  3. Promote Positivity: Encourage healthy habits and a balanced lifestyle.
  4. Use Explanatory Language: Clearly describe the benefits and methods of well-being.
  5. Speak with Clarity and Conviction: Convey the importance of the topic.

Vocabulary Builder: Words for Health and Well-being

Let's build your word bank! Remember to write these down in your notebook, and add any new ones you learn!

Aspects of Health:

  • Physical health: The condition of your body.
  • Mental health: Your emotional, psychological, and social well-being.
  • Emotional well-being: Your ability to manage and express emotions.
  • Holistic health: An approach to health that considers the whole person (body, mind, spirit).
  • Nutrition: The process of providing or obtaining the food necessary for health and growth.
  • Exercise: Activity requiring physical effort, carried out to sustain or improve health and fitness.
  • Sleep hygiene: Practices necessary to have normal quality nighttime sleep and full daytime alertness.
  • Stress management: Techniques for controlling a person's level of stress.
  • Mindfulness: A mental state achieved by focusing one's awareness on the present moment.
  • Immune system: The body's defense against infection.
  • Metabolism: The chemical processes that occur within a living organism in order to maintain life.

Healthy Practices & Benefits:

  • Balanced diet: A diet that contains the proper proportions of carbohydrates, fats, proteins, vitamins, minerals, and water.
  • Hydration: The process of causing something to absorb water.
  • Regular physical activity: Consistent engagement in exercise.
  • Adequate sleep: Getting enough rest.
  • Mind-body connection: The link between your thoughts, feelings, and physical health.
  • Preventative care: Measures taken for disease prevention.
  • Boost: To improve or increase.
  • Enhance: To intensify, increase, or further improve the quality, value, or extent of.
  • Reduce: To make smaller or less in amount, degree, or size.
  • Improve: To make or become better.
  • Cope with: To deal effectively with something difficult.

Grammar Focus: Explaining Benefits and Giving Advice

These structures will help you explain the advantages of healthy practices and offer advice:

Expressing Purpose/Benefit:

  • [Action] in order to [benefit]. (e.g., "Regular exercise is crucial in order to maintain physical health.")
  • [Action] helps to [benefit]. (e.g., "Mindfulness helps to reduce stress.")
  • [Action] contributes to [benefit]. (e.g., "A balanced diet contributes to a strong immune system.")
  • [Action] is essential for [benefit].

Giving Advice/Recommendations:

  • You should/shouldn't...
  • It's advisable to...
  • I recommend that you...
  • It's important to...
  • One effective way to [achieve goal] is to...

Using Gerunds as Subjects:

  • [Gerund] is beneficial for [purpose]. (e.g., "Eating nutritious food is beneficial for overall well-being.")
  • [Gerund] can improve [aspect].

Speaking Practice: Discussing Healthy Habits

  1. Choose a Health Aspect: Think about one aspect of health and well-being that you find important (e.g., physical fitness, mental resilience, healthy eating).
  2. Describe Healthy Habits: What specific habits or practices contribute to this aspect of health?
  3. Explain Benefits: How do these habits boost, enhance, or improve one's well-being?
  4. Give Advice: What advice would you give to someone looking to improve this area of their health?
  5. Discuss: Practice discussing your chosen health aspect and related habits. Set a timer for 2-3 minutes for this practice. Try to use:
  • At least 3-4 new vocabulary words from the list.
  • At least 1-2 sentences expressing purpose/benefit.
  • Phrases for giving advice.
