Homework Assignments

To help you master discussing and writing about career exploration:

  1. Speaking Practice:
  • Interview a family member, teacher, or friend about their career path. Ask them about their skill set, challenges, and what they enjoy most about their profession.
  • Share what you learned with another person, using at least 5 new words or phrases from this lesson.
  1. Vocabulary Journal:
  • Review your notebook. Choose your top 5 favorite new words from today and write one original sentence for each, related to careers.
  • Try to make at least two of these sentences use structures for expressing future plans or discussing requirements.
  1. Complete the Reflective Essay:
  • Finish writing your short reflective essay on future career interests (aim for about 250-350 words).
  • Focus on genuine reflection, clear articulation of interests and goals, and include at least 10-12 of the new career terms and relevant phrases.
  1. Proofread & Edit:
  • Carefully proofread your essay for grammar, spelling, punctuation, and clarity.
  • Read it aloud to catch awkward phrasing or errors.
  1. Skill Assessment:
  • In your notebook, list 3-5 skills you currently possess (e.g., problem-solving, communication, creativity).
  • For each skill, briefly explain how it might be useful in a future profession you're considering.

Remember your notebook! Continue to use it actively. Write down new words, good sample sentences, and any tips that help you improve your speaking and writing. This will be a valuable resource for your test!

Lesson 11: Global Citizenship

Hello students! In this lesson, we're going to explore the concept of global citizenship. This means understanding our roles and responsibilities in an interconnected world, addressing global challenges, and promoting positive change beyond national borders. By the end of this lesson, you'll be able to discuss global responsibilities and write persuasive essays on global initiatives.
