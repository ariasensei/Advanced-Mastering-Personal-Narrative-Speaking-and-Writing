Homework Assignments

To help you master discussing and writing about cultural diversity:

  1. Speaking Practice:
  • Discuss a cultural custom or tradition from your own country with a family member or friend.
  • Try to explain its significance and perhaps compare it to something similar or different in another culture, using at least 5 new words or phrases related to cultural diversity.
  1. Vocabulary Journal:
  • Review your notebook. Choose your top 5 favorite new words from today and write one original sentence for each, related to culture.
  • Try to make at least two of these sentences use comparative structures (e.g., "more...than," "whereas").
  1. Complete the Descriptive Essay:
  • Finish writing your descriptive essay on a cultural celebration (aim for about 250-350 words).
  • Focus on rich descriptive language, engaging sensory details, and include at least 10-12 of the new cultural terms and comparative phrases.
  1. Proofread & Edit:
  • Carefully proofread your essay for grammar, spelling, punctuation, and clarity.
  • Read it aloud to catch awkward phrasing or errors.
  1. Cultural Observation/Research:
  • Choose a country different from your own. Research one of its unique cultural values or norms.
  • In your notebook, jot down 3-5 key points about this value/norm and how it might differ from your own culture.

Remember your notebook! Continue to use it actively. Write down new words, good sample sentences, and any tips that help you improve your speaking and writing. This will be a valuable resource for your test!

Lesson 5: Problem-Solving and Decision-Making

Hello students! In this lesson, we're going to focus on problem-solving and decision-making. This involves learning how to identify problems, analyze potential solutions, and make informed choices. By the end of this lesson, you'll be able to discuss hypothetical problems, propose solutions, and justify your decisions clearly.
