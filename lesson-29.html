Part 1: Speaking About Careers

Your Speaking Guides

When discussing career exploration, these guides will help you:

  1. Be Reflective: Think deeply about your own strengths, weaknesses, and passions.
  2. Be Realistic: Consider the requirements and challenges of different professions.
  3. Ask Questions: Be curious about various career paths.
  4. Use Specifics: Refer to particular job roles, industries, or skills.
  5. Speak with Enthusiasm and Purpose: Convey your interest in your future.

Vocabulary Builder: Words for Career Exploration

Let's build your word bank! Remember to write these down in your notebook, and add any new ones you learn!

Career Concepts:

  • Career path: A sequence of jobs that constitute a person's professional life.
  • Profession: A paid occupation, especially one that involves prolonged training and a formal qualification.
  • Industry: A group of businesses that provide a particular product or service.
  • Skill set: A person's range of skills or abilities.
  • Aptitude: A natural ability to do something.
  • Interest: The feeling of wanting to know or learn about something or someone.
  • Passion: A strong and barely controllable emotion. (Often used for strong enthusiasm for a subject/activity).
  • Job market: The number of jobs that are available in a particular place or for a particular type of work.
  • Internship: The position of a student or trainee who works in an organization, sometimes without pay, in order to gain work experience or satisfy requirements for a qualification.
  • Mentorship: The guidance provided by a mentor, especially an experienced person in a company or educational institution.
  • Networking: Interact with other people to exchange information and develop professional or social contacts.
  • Entrepreneurship: The activity of setting up a business or businesses, taking on financial risks in the hope of profit.
  • Vocational training: Training for a specific trade or craft.

Personal Qualities & Actions:

  • Dedicated: Devoted to a task or purpose.
  • Ambitious: Having or showing a strong desire and determination to succeed.
  • Adaptable: Able to adjust to new conditions.
  • Resilient: Able to withstand or recover quickly from difficult conditions.
  • Proactive: Creating or controlling a situation rather than just responding to it after it has happened.
  • Pursue: To follow or try to achieve (something, such as a goal) over a period of time.
  • Specialize in: To concentrate on a particular activity or product.
  • Gain experience: To acquire knowledge or skill through practice.
  • Develop skills: To improve abilities.
  • Set goals: To establish aims or objectives.

Grammar Focus: Expressing Future Plans and Qualifications

These structures will help you discuss your career aspirations and what's needed to achieve them:

Expressing Future Plans (Review & Expansion):

  • I plan to [verb]...
  • I hope to [verb]...
  • I aspire to be a [profession]...
  • My goal is to [verb]...
  • I envision myself [gerund]...
  • I am considering [gerund]...

Discussing Requirements/Qualifications:

  • To become a [profession], one needs to [requirement]. (e.g., "To become a doctor, one needs to complete extensive medical training.")
  • It is essential to have [skill] for this role.
  • A strong [aptitude] is crucial in this field.
  • This career path requires [qualification/experience].

Using Gerunds and Infinitives for Purpose:

  • I am studying [subject] to [purpose]. (e.g., "I am studying science to gain a deeper understanding of the human body.")
  • [Action] is key for [purpose]. (e.g., "Networking is key for building professional contacts.")

Speaking Practice: Discussing Career Paths

  1. Choose a Career Path: Think about a career path that interests you, or one you know something about.
  2. Describe the Role: What does someone in this profession do? What industry is it in?
  3. Discuss Requirements: What skill set, aptitude, or qualifications are needed?
  4. Explain Your Interest (if applicable): If it's a career you're considering, explain why it interests you and what passion you have for it.
  5. Discuss: Practice discussing your chosen career path. Set a timer for 2-3 minutes for this practice. Try to use:
  • At least 3-4 new vocabulary words from the list.
  • At least 1-2 sentences expressing future plans or requirements.
  • Phrases to explain your interests.
