Part 1: Speaking About Your Life

Your Speaking Guides

When you share a personal story or your dreams, think of these guides:

  1. Be Clear: Make sure your listener can easily follow your story or understand your goals.
  2. Use Details: Add interesting details that make your story come alive.
  3. Connect Your Ideas: Use special words to link your sentences and ideas smoothly.
  4. Speak with Confidence: Don't be afraid to share your thoughts!

Vocabulary Builder: Words for Your Stories and Dreams

Let's build your word bank! Here are some words and phrases you can use. Remember to write these down in your notebook, and add any new ones you learn!

Describing Experiences:

  • Memorable: Something you'll always remember.
  • Challenging: Something difficult that tested you.
  • Joyful: Something that made you very happy.
  • Impactful: Something that had a big effect on you.
  • Unforgettable: Impossible to forget.
  • Turning point: A moment when things changed significantly.
  • Overcome: To successfully deal with a problem.
  • Learned a lesson: Gained new knowledge or understanding from an experience.
  • Realized: Understood something suddenly.
  • Discovered: Found something new or unexpected.

Talking About Aspirations (Your Hopes & Dreams for the Future):

  • Aim to: What you intend to do.
  • Hope to: What you wish for.
  • Aspire to: To have a strong desire to achieve something.
  • Envision myself: How you imagine yourself in the future.
  • Passionate about: Having a strong feeling or belief in something.
  • Goal: Something you want to achieve.
  • Objective: A specific aim or target.
  • Pursue: To follow or try to achieve something over a period of time.
  • Strive for: To make great efforts to achieve something.
  • Dream of: To imagine something you want to happen.
  • Ultimately: In the end; finally.
  • Long-term: Over a long period of time.
  • Short-term: Over a short period of time.

Grammar Focus: Connecting Your Ideas (Transition Words)

These words help your story flow smoothly from one idea to the next:

  • To start: First, To begin with, Initially
  • To continue: Then, Next, After that, Meanwhile, Subsequently
  • To show cause/effect: As a result, Consequently, Therefore, Due to this
  • To conclude: Finally, In conclusion, Ultimately, To sum up
  • To add information: Furthermore, Moreover, In addition, Also
  • To show contrast: However, On the other hand, But, Although

Practice: Try to use these in your sentences. For example:

  • "First, I woke up early. Then, I went for a run. After that, I had breakfast."
  • "I aspire to be a doctor. However, I know it will be a lot of hard work."

Speaking Practice: Tell Your Story!

  1. Choose an Experience: Think about one significant event or experience from your life. It could be:
  • A funny moment
  • A time you learned something important
  • A challenging situation you overcame
  • A memorable trip or event
  1. Plan Your Story (Mentally):
  • Introduction: Briefly set the scene (who, what, where, when).
  • Rising Action: What happened leading up to the main event?
  • Climax: The most important part of the story.
  • Falling Action: What happened immediately after the climax?
  • Resolution/Reflection: What was the outcome, or what did you learn?
  1. Share Your Aspirations: Think about what you want to achieve in the future.
  • What are your academic goals?
  • What kind of career do you envision for yourself?
  • What personal dreams do you have?

Your Turn: Practice telling your story and sharing your aspirations aloud. Set a timer for 2-3 minutes for this practice. Try to use some of the new vocabulary and transition words!
