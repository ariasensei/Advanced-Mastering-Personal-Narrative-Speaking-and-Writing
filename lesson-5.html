Part 1: Speaking About Global Challenges

Your Speaking Guides

When discussing global issues, these guides will help you:

  1. Use Formal Language: Try to use more formal words and phrases, like those you might find in a news article or academic paper.
  2. State Your Opinion Clearly: Let your listener know what you believe or think about the issue.
  3. Explain Cause and Effect: Show how one thing leads to another (e.g., "pollution leads to health problems").
  4. Propose Solutions: Don't just talk about problems; think about how they can be fixed!
  5. Listen and Respond: Be ready to discuss different viewpoints.

Vocabulary Builder: Words for Environmental Issues

Let's learn some important words for discussing the environment. Remember to write these down in your notebook and add more as you learn them!

Environmental Challenges:

  • Climate change: A long-term shift in global weather patterns.
  • Global warming: The gradual increase in the Earth's average temperature.
  • Deforestation: The clearing of forests for other land uses.
  • Pollution: Harmful substances introduced into the environment (e.g., air pollution, water pollution, plastic pollution).
  • Biodiversity loss: The reduction of different types of life on Earth.
  • Sustainability: Meeting our own needs without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own needs.
  • Carbon footprint: The total amount of greenhouse gases produced by human activities.
  • Greenhouse gases: Gases that trap heat in the atmosphere (e.g., carbon dioxide).
  • Natural resources depletion: Using up natural resources faster than they can be replaced.

Solutions and Actions:

  • Renewable energy: Energy from sources that are naturally replenished (e.g., solar, wind).
  • Conservation: Protecting and preserving natural resources and the environment.
  • Recycling: Converting waste into reusable material.
  • Waste management: The collection, transport, processing, and disposal of waste materials.
  • Reforestation: The process of replanting trees in areas where forests have been removed.
  • Advocacy: Public support for or recommendation of a particular cause or policy.
  • Policy: A course or principle of action adopted or proposed by a government, party, business, or individual.
  • Sustainable practices: Ways of doing things that don't harm the environment or deplete resources.
  • Technological innovation: New inventions or methods that solve problems.
  • Reduce, reuse, recycle: The "3 Rs" of waste management.

Grammar Focus: Discussing Complex Ideas

These phrases and structures will help you express more complex thoughts:

Expressing Opinion/Stance:

  • I believe that...
  • In my opinion,...
  • It seems to me that...
  • From my perspective,...
  • I am convinced that...

Showing Cause and Effect:

  • Due to [cause], [effect] occurred. (e.g., "Due to deforestation, many animal habitats are lost.")
  • [Cause] leads to [effect]. (e.g., "Air pollution leads to respiratory problems.")
  • As a result of [cause], [effect] happened.
  • Consequently, [effect] occurred.
  • [Effect] stems from [cause].

Proposing Solutions:

  • One potential solution is to...
  • It's crucial to...
  • We must consider...
  • Implementing [solution] could...
  • It's imperative that we...

Using Complex Sentences (with conjunctions):

  • Although [idea 1], [idea 2]. (e.g., "Although climate change is a global problem, individual actions can still make a difference.")
  • Even though [idea 1], [idea 2].
  • While [idea 1], [idea 2].
  • Because [reason], [result].
  • Since [reason], [result].
  • If [condition], [result].
  • In order to [goal], we must [action].

Speaking Practice: Discussing Environmental Issues

  1. Choose a Challenge: What do you think is the most pressing environmental challenge facing our world today? (e.g., plastic pollution, global warming, deforestation).
  2. Explain Why: Why is this challenge so important? What are its causes and effects?
  3. Propose Solutions: What can individuals do? What can governments or large organizations do?
  4. Discuss: Practice discussing your chosen challenge and solutions. Set a timer for 2-3 minutes for this practice. Try to use:
  • At least 3-4 new vocabulary words from the list.
  • At least 1-2 complex sentences using the structures above.
  • Phrases to express your opinion and propose solutions.
