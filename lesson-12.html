Part 2: Writing About Culture

Your Writing Guides

When writing about cultural diversity, your writing should be:

  1. Descriptive: Paint a vivid picture with words.
  2. Informative: Provide clear and accurate details.
  3. Respectful: Maintain an appreciative and unbiased tone.
  4. Organized: Use paragraphs to separate different aspects of your description.

Writing Structure: The Descriptive Essay on a Cultural Celebration

This structure will help you write a clear and engaging descriptive essay:

  • Introduction (1 paragraph):
  • Hook: Start with a captivating sentence about the beauty of cultural diversity or the specific celebration.
  • Background: Introduce the cultural celebration you will describe (name, where it's celebrated, when).
  • Thesis Statement (or controlling idea): Briefly state what makes this celebration unique or significant.
  • Example Thesis: "The Ati-Atihan Festival in Aklan, Philippines, is a vibrant and unforgettable celebration of faith and heritage, characterized by its rhythmic dances and colorful costumes."
  • Body Paragraph 1: Sensory Details (Sight & Sound) (1 paragraph):
  • Topic Sentence: Focus on what one sees and hears during the celebration.
  • Elaboration: Describe the visual elements (costumes, decorations, parades) and auditory elements (music, chants, crowd sounds) using vivid adjectives and adverbs.
  • Example: "The streets of Kalibo come alive with a kaleidoscope of colors as participants, adorned in distinctive tribal costumes, dance to the insistent beat of drums. The air is filled with the joyous shouts of 'Hala Bira!'"
  • Body Paragraph 2: Sensory Details (Feelings & Atmosphere) (1 paragraph):
  • Topic Sentence: Focus on the atmosphere, emotions, and perhaps other senses (smell, taste) associated with the celebration.
  • Elaboration: Describe the energy, the feeling of community, the smells of food, or any other sensory experiences that contribute to the overall ambiance.
  • Example: "An overwhelming sense of camaraderie and devotion permeates the festival, as locals and tourists alike join in the revelry. The aroma of street food mingles with the festive spirit, creating an immersive cultural experience."
  • Conclusion (1 paragraph):
  • Summarize: Briefly recap the key descriptive points.
  • Reflect: Explain the significance of the celebration to the culture or what it taught you about cultural diversity.
  • Final Thought: End with a powerful statement about the beauty or importance of cultural traditions.

Writing Practice: Your Cultural Celebration Essay

  1. Outline Your Essay: Choose a cultural celebration or tradition that you can describe in detail. It could be from your own culture or one you've learned about. Create a quick outline for a short essay (2-3 paragraphs) following the structure above.
  2. Draft Your Essay: Start writing your descriptive essay. Focus on:
  • Using vivid descriptive language.
  • Engaging the reader's senses.
  • Organizing your ideas logically.
  • Incorporating the new vocabulary.
