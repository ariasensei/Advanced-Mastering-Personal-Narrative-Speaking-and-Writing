Part 1: Speaking About the Future

Your Speaking Guides

When discussing innovation and future trends, these guides will help you:

  1. Be Speculative: Use language that suggests possibilities and predictions, rather than certainties.
  2. Support Predictions: Base your ideas on current trends, scientific advancements, or logical reasoning.
  3. Consider Multiple Scenarios: Think about both optimistic and pessimistic possibilities.
  4. Use Forward-Looking Language: Employ vocabulary and grammar that refer to future events and developments.
  5. Speak with Imagination and Curiosity: Convey your excitement about what's to come.

Vocabulary Builder: Words for Innovation and Future Trends

Let's build your word bank! Remember to write these down in your notebook, and add any new ones you learn!

Innovation & Progress:

  • Innovation: A new method, idea, product, etc.
  • Breakthrough: A sudden, dramatic, and important discovery or development.
  • Pioneering: Involving new ideas or methods; innovative or groundbreaking.
  • Cutting-edge: Highly advanced; innovative.
  • Emerging technology: New technologies that are currently developing or will be developed in the next five to ten years.
  • Disruptive technology: An innovation that significantly alters the way that consumers, industries, or businesses operate.
  • Advancement: A development or improvement.
  • Progress: Forward or onward movement toward a destination.
  • Evolution: The gradual development of something.
  • Revolutionize: To change (something) fundamentally and completely.

Future Trends & Predictions:

  • Trend: A general direction in which something is developing or changing.
  • Forecast: To predict or estimate (a future event or development).
  • Speculate: To form a theory or conjecture about a subject without firm evidence.
  • Predict: To state that (an event or development) will happen in the future or will be a consequence of something.
  • Envision: To imagine as a future possibility; visualize.
  • Scenario: A sequence of possible events.
  • Futuristic: Having or involving very modern technology or design.
  • Sustainable future: A future where human well-being is achieved without depleting natural resources.
  • Demographic shift: A change in the characteristics of a population.
  • Paradigm shift: A fundamental change in approach or underlying assumptions.

Grammar Focus: Expressing Future Possibility and Speculation

These structures will help you effectively discuss future trends and make predictions:

Expressing Possibility/Probability:

  • It is likely that...
  • It is probable that...
  • There is a strong possibility that...
  • We might/may see...
  • Could potentially lead to...
  • Is expected to...

Using Future Tenses (Review & Expansion):

  • Will + base verb: For definite predictions (e.g., "AI will continue to advance rapidly.").
  • Be going to + base verb: For plans or strong intentions (e.g., "Governments are going to invest more in renewable energy.").
  • Future Continuous: For ongoing actions in the future (e.g., "By 2050, autonomous vehicles will be driving on most roads.").
  • Future Perfect: For actions completed by a certain time in the future (e.g., "By the end of the century, humanity will have achieved significant breakthroughs in space travel.").

Speculative Language:

  • I speculate that...
  • One could envision a future where...
  • It is conceivable that...
  • Perhaps, in the coming decades,...
  • What if...? (For posing hypothetical questions)

Speaking Practice: Discussing Future Predictions

  1. Choose a Future Trend: Select an area where you anticipate significant innovation or change (e.g., transportation, education, medicine, communication, energy).
  2. Predict Developments: What breakthroughs or advancements do you forecast in this area?
  3. Discuss Impact: How might these changes revolutionize society? What are the potential benefits and drawbacks?
  4. Speculate on Scenarios: What kind of futuristic world do you envision as a result?
  5. Discuss: Practice discussing your chosen future trend and predictions. Set a timer for 2-3 minutes for this practice. Try to use:
  • At least 3-4 new vocabulary words from the list.
  • At least 1-2 sentences using future perfect or future continuous.
  • Phrases for expressing possibility and speculation.
