Part 2: Writing About Global Citizenship

Your Writing Guides

When writing about global citizenship, your writing should be:

  1. Persuasive: Convince the reader of the importance of your argument.
  2. Argumentative: Present a clear thesis and support it with logical reasoning and evidence.
  3. Global-Minded: Demonstrate an understanding of interconnectedness and global implications.
  4. Structured: Follow a clear essay format.

Writing Structure: The Persuasive Essay on a Global Initiative

This structure will help you write a clear and compelling persuasive essay:

  • Title: Impactful and clear (e.g., "The Imperative of Global Collaboration in Addressing the Climate Crisis").
  • Introduction (1 paragraph):
  • Hook: Start with a powerful statement about the interconnectedness of the world or a pressing global issue.
  • Background: Briefly introduce the global challenge you will focus on and the need for collective action.
  • Thesis Statement: Clearly state your main argument – what specific global initiative or approach is essential, and why?
  • Example Thesis: "As global citizens, it is our collective responsibility to actively address the escalating climate crisis through enhanced international collaboration and the widespread adoption of sustainable development practices."
  • Body Paragraph 1: Defining the Global Challenge (1 paragraph):
  • Topic Sentence: Elaborate on the chosen global challenge.
  • Elaboration: Provide details about its causes, its global consequences, and why it requires urgent attention. Use relevant vocabulary.
  • Example: "The climate crisis presents an existential threat, with rising sea levels and extreme weather events impacting communities worldwide. This issue stems from decades of unsustainable industrial practices, highlighting our interconnectedness."
  • Body Paragraph 2: Proposing and Justifying the Initiative (1 paragraph):
  • Topic Sentence: Introduce your proposed global initiative or solution.
  • Elaboration: Explain how this initiative would work, why it is effective, and what positive outcomes it would bring. Emphasize the role of collaboration and accountability.
  • Example: "Enhanced international collaboration is imperative to mitigate the effects of climate change. Governments must work together to establish stringent environmental policies and invest in renewable energy technologies, demonstrating their accountability to future generations."
  • Conclusion (1 paragraph):
  • Summarize: Briefly restate the global challenge and the importance of your proposed initiative.
  • Reiterate Thesis: Rephrase your thesis statement.
  • Final Call to Action: End with a strong, motivating statement about the role of global citizens in shaping a better future.

Writing Practice: Your Global Initiative Essay

  1. Outline Your Essay: Choose a specific global challenge and a corresponding initiative or approach you believe is vital (e.g., promoting human rights, achieving social justice, combating poverty, fostering sustainable development). Create a quick outline for a short persuasive essay (2-3 paragraphs) following the structure above.
  2. Draft Your Essay: Start writing your persuasive essay. Focus on:
  • Clearly stating your argument.
  • Supporting your claims with logical reasoning.
  • Using formal and persuasive language.
  • Demonstrating a global perspective.
