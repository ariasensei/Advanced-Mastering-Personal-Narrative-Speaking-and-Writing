Part 2: Writing Your Life

Your Writing Guides

Writing is like speaking, but on paper! Here are some guides for writing:

  1. Structure is Key: Organize your ideas with an introduction, body paragraphs, and a conclusion.
  2. Descriptive Language: Use words that help the reader "see" and "feel" what you're describing.
  3. Clear Sentences: Make sure each sentence is easy to understand.
  4. Proofread: Always read your writing carefully to catch any mistakes.

Writing Structure: The Narrative Essay

For your personal narrative, follow this structure:

  • Introduction:
  • Grab the reader's attention.
  • Briefly introduce the experience you're going to write about.
  • Body Paragraphs (2-3 paragraphs):
  • Tell your story in order.
  • Each paragraph can focus on a different part of the event (e.g., the beginning, the main challenge, the resolution).
  • Use descriptive words and transition words to connect your ideas.
  • Conclusion:
  • Summarize the main points of your story.
  • Explain what you learned from the experience or how it impacted you.
  • (Optional) Connect it to your future aspirations.

Writing Practice: Put It On Paper!

  1. Outline Your Narrative: Before you write, quickly jot down the main points for each section of your story.
  2. Draft Your Narrative: Start writing your personal narrative. Don't worry about perfection on the first try! Focus on getting your ideas down, using the vocabulary, and following the structure.
  3. Write About Your Aspirations: In a separate paragraph, describe your top 2-3 future aspirations. Explain why these goals are important to you.
